Understand the Impact


Gun violence is a complex problem with wide-reaching consequences. To address it effectively, we must first grasp the extent of its impact. Below, we present important statistics that reveal the magnitude of the issue.


Gun Violence disproportionately impacts marginalized identities in America


Each year in America, approximately 10,000 African Americans die due to firearms as a result of gun violence. African Americans only make up 14% of America's population (US Census Bureau), yet they compose 59% of victims of firearm related homicide.


Approximately 3,800 Latinos die from gun violence every year in America. The firearm homicide rate is four times higher for Hispanic male youth in comparison to non-Hispanic youth of the same age.


Every year in America, there are an average of 10,000 hate crimes that involve the usage of firearms. Approximately a fifth of hate crimes committed in America target the LGBTQ+ community based on sexual identity or gender identity bias.


America has the highest rate of child and teen mortality due to firearms among peer countries


The firearm mortality rate of children has doubled in America since 2013. In 2020 and 2021, firearms were involved in the deaths of children from the ages of 1-17 more than any other illness or injury.


In America, teenagers ages 18 and 19 had a firearm mortality rate of 25.2 per 100,000 in 2021. The firearm death rate among teenages is over 9.5 times the firearm death rate of teenagers in Canada.


Gun Violence negatively impacts mental health


Frequent exposure to such high rates of gun violence and firearms is linked to many negative mental health outcomes, such as depression, suicidal ideation, and psychological distress in individuals of various backgrounds.
